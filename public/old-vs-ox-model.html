Diagram 9 — Section 6 · What Is OX
A different architecture to every existing marketplace.
The Old Model
Centralised. Extractive. Platform takes the value.
Centralised data ownership
Platform holds all participant data centrally — owned by the company, not the individual
Platform extracts value from participants
Every gig platform, professional network, and task exchange takes a cut — participants are the product
Ratings and reputation scores control access
Historical behaviour determines who gets work — not verified capability
Platform owns the supply-demand relationship
The intermediary sits between every buyer and seller — and charges accordingly
Data can be hacked, sold, or subpoenaed
Central databases are single points of failure for privacy, security, and trust
Participants have no sovereignty
You participate on the platform's terms — your data, your identity, their asset
VS
The OX Model
Decentralised. Consent-based. Value flows back to participants.
Data never leaves the participant's device
Every Machine Self lives on the individual's phone — local, sovereign, unhackable at source
Value flows back through the token economy
Tokens earned by participants — not extracted by a platform intermediary
Matching based on verified, declared capability
2,000 human-created data points — not ratings, scores, or historical behaviour
OX replaces the architecture entirely
OX does not compete with existing platforms. It replaces the architecture they are all built on
One billion sovereign nodes — no central failure point
No database to hack, sell, or subpoena. Each Machine Self owned by the individual it represents
Participants are sovereign economic actors
You own your Machine Self. You control your data. You set your consent. You earn the value.
Old architecture: every existing human coordination marketplace extracts value from participants and holds their data centrally.
OX architecture: structured participation, not surveillance. Value flows back to the people who built the network.
